The story of Eddie (Son) James House, Jr. begins on March 21, 1902 in Riverton, MS

1917

By the young age of fifteen, Son House followed his father's example, as Eddie House Sr. was a Baptist deacon, and had started preaching.

1921

Son House married a woman called Carrie Martin but left her after a few hard months when his mother died.

1927

After hopping from job to job, Son House listened to a man play bottleneck blues and went out to buy a guitar at 25 years old.

1930

After serving two years in jail for shooting a man in self-defense, Son House met the famous Charley Patton and his partner Willie Brown.

1930

After joining with Patton and Brown, the trio was approached and recorded by Paramount Records.

1934

After Charlie Patton dies, Son House continued to play with Willie Brown and become the two leading musicians of Coahoma Country.

In Coahoma Country, three young emerging musicians - Robert Johnson, Muddy Waters, and Howlin' Wolf - are inspired and mentored by Son House. They would go on to become some of the greatest to every play the blues, influencing millions of guitarists.

1941

1942

Son House and his band finished the infamous Liberty of Congress recordings.

1943

Son House moved to Rochester, New York and retires.

1964

Son House gets rediscovered in the Blues Revival by a group of young vinyl collectors and is re-taught to play the guitar by Alan Wilson.

Over the next couple of years, Son House would go on several tours, record the "Father of Delta Blues: The Complete 1965 Sessions," and play in prominent festivals such as the Days of Blues Festival in Toronto.

1974

Son House retired again after being afflicted by health issues.

1980

Son Houses gets inducted into the Blues Foundation's Hall of Fame.

1988

The great Son House dies on October 19, 1988 at 88 years old.
